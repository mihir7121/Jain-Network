Foreword

  • By Dr Satish R Ranka, Occupational Physician Communications Officer: Jain Network

COVID-19 was declared a Pandemic by World Health Organisation in March 2020. It is also a prescribed occupational disease. It is a group 3-4 biological hazard agent which causes severe human disease. This new infectious disease affects the respiratory system. It causes severe pneumonia which is life-threatening and difficult to treat. Spread of infection can be prevented by social distancing, hand hygiene and use of face coverings. United Kingdom government has approved two doses of Pfizer BioNTech COVID-19 mRNA vaccine BNT162b2 to combat this.

Jain network, London is pleased to release this booklet providing a brief summary of COVID-19 disease. This booklet provides information on the virus causing COVID-19, steps to prevent the spread of the disease, restrictions according to the tier system, information on vulnerable groups, face coverings and vaccination.

For full information please visit UK government website

Hazard

  • Infection with Corona Virus (SARS-CoV-2)

Risk

  • Development of COVID-19 disease

Control

  • Social distancing: minimum 2 meters
  • Hand Hygiene: Wash hands for 20 seconds
  • Face Covering

Pandemic

When an infectious disease spreads through a large region, continents or worldwide e.g. Swine Flu pandemic in 2009 and the Ebola Virus pandemic in 2014.

Virus

A microorganism which can only live and reproduce in a host body. They are smaller than bacteria and are passed on from one living being to another (humans, birds, and animals). Example: HIV, Hepatitis B, Measles, Mumps, Polio virus.

Vaccine

A vaccine is a kind of medicine which helps the body’s immune system to recognise and destroy living microorganisms during future infection. It can contain a dead or weakened version of the microorganism.

Introduction

COVID-19 is caused by a virus called as the Coronavirus SARS-CoV-2. It is an RNA virus. It is spread through droplet infection through secretions from the lungs by coughing or sneezing. It is also spread by direct human contact and fomites. Symptoms appear after 2-14 days of exposure to the virus. It causes severe pneumonia. It can also cause sepsis, septic shock, heart disease, clots in arteries and veins and failure of multiple organs in the body.

NHS Track and Trace

Helps with testing of people with COVID-19 symptoms and asymptomatic NHS and care staff. It helps to track close contacts of a positive case of Corona virus and notifies them to self-isolate. A combination of the above two, is likely to control the R number.


R number

Reproduction number i.e. if R number is 1, 1 person with infection can infect 1 another person. The idea is to keep the R number to as low as possible preferably below 1.


Self-Isolation

Current advice is to self-isolate for 10 days if one has COVID-19 symptoms or from the day of a positive test in asymptomatic people.


Face coverings:

Fabric coverings Surgical mask
For source control:

Covid 19: 3 Categories

Acute COVID-19 Long COVID-19
Ongoing symptomatic COVID-19 Post COVID-19 Syndrome
COVID-19 symptoms < 4 weeks Ongoing symptoms for 4-12 weeks. Ongoing COVID-19 symptoms for > 12 weeks

Vaccination

K government has approved 2 two doses of Pfizer vaccine. The vaccine doses are given three weeks apart. This vaccine is 52% effective after first dose and 95% effective after a second dose. Immunity starts to develop 12 days after the first dose. It will first be given to people above age 80, people working in care homes and healthcare workers at high risk.

Common side effects include mild injection site pain and tenderness, short lived fatigue, headache, and mild fever. Anaphylaxis (severe allergic reaction) is rare.

COVID 19 vaccine does not contain any animal products or egg.
