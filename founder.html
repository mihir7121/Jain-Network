Natubhai Shah MBE

Founder - Jain Network

  • 165 Township Line Road, Suite 1200 Jenkintown, PA 19046

  • 123 456 789 09

  • username@domain.org

Birth

Natubhai was born in Palanpur in Gujrat in India. Palanpur is the ancestral home to an industry of Indian diamond merchants. According to Jain texts, Palanpur was founded in 1218 by rulers of Paramara Dynasty.

Career

Natubhai was a General Practitioner in India for 11years and for 28years in Leicester. Natubhai retired in 1996, took early retirement on pronciples of Jainism: aparigraha to devote full time to academic, social and charitable work. Currently, Natubhai is a Fellow of Royal Society of Medicine, London

Positions Held

Natubhai is renowned for his social, religious and interfaith activities. He held a number of positions throughout his career and after retirement.

  • Chairman of Jain Network London. Natubhai was also the chairman of the Council of Dharmic Faiths, Barnet Hindu Forum, Co- Chairman of Jain-Jewish Association & Jain-Christian Association, Vice-chairman of Barnet Multi-Faith Forum and Co-coordinator of London Council of Jain Organizations.
  • He was also a Trustee of Inter-Faith Network UK and International Interfaith Centre Oxford from 1997-2000.
  • He is currently trustee of Colindale Community development and a member of the graham park consortium.

Education

Natubhai is from the 1951 batch of Grant Medical College, Mumbai. He practised medicine for 10 years in India. 80% of his batchmates went abroad to study and work, Natubhai came to the UK in 1968.

Philosophy

Natubhai believes in promotion of good community relations, being helpful to the communities irrespective of caste, creed, colour or gender.

He follows the motto of Jainism ‘Live and help to live’

The goal of his life is to promote Jainism, one of the world’s oldest Indian religions, which provides a code of conduct based on ahimsaa, aparigraha and anekaantavaad and a way of life which rejects violence, self-seeking and dishonesty.

Its philosophy and teaching of Peace, Love and Harmony - individual freedom, equality regardless of race, sex, caste and colour, reverence for all life, human and animal welfare, concern for the environment, pluralism and spiritual liberation by self-effort - a need in​ today's troubled World. His wife Bhanuben, was his anchor.

The Awards




Delhi Jain Mahasabha

In 1979, Natubhai along with his partner won the Leicestershire Gimson Trophy defeating 600 pairs in duplicate bridge.

In 1987 the Vice President of India- Shankar Dayal Sharma presented Natubhai an award from Delhi Jain Mahasabha.

In 1988 Natubhai was honoured by the Jain Organizations of UK for the dedicated work of creating the Leicester Jain Centre.

In 1989 he was honoured by the Jain Community when he stepped down from the post of the President of Jain Samaj Europe after 12 years dedicated service.

The Man of the Year


In 1990 Natubhai was honoured as ‘An International Leaders in Achievement’ by the International Biographical Centre Cambridge for an outstanding contribution to community work and promotion of Jainism in the West.

In 1991 the American Biographical Institute Board of International Research chose Natubhai ‘The Man of the Year’ based on the outstanding accomplishments and noble example set for peers and the entire community.

In a premier function organized to celebrate the 2600th birth anniversary of Bhagwan Mahavira at Mumbai on 8th April 2001, the Prime Minister of India awarded the highest Jain award the ‘JAIN RATNA’ to Natubhai for his outstanding contribution for promotion of Jainism.

The Leicester Journey

On arrival into the UK, Natubhai settled in Leicester in 1968. He practised as a general practitioner and within one year he established his own practice in Leicester. Whilst working for standing conference of Asian organisation as a treasurer and Indian national club as vice chairman in Leicester and Gujrati Organisation, it transpired him to bring the Jain Community on world map. This was a remarkable achievement and also the steppingstone for his Jainism Journey. Natubhai became the chairman of Jain Samaj Leicester in 1977 and put Leicester on the Jain map of the world. Jain samah Leicester was registered as a charity in 1978 with a vison of a Jain Centre, where all major Jain traditions can study and worship under one roof.

In 1979, a land was purchased in the heart of Leicester and Natubhai helped to build a rapport with the local community in Leicester, local and national Interfaith organisations, Jains in the UK, Antwerp, India and other parts of the world and also the media. Jain Centre obtained substantial grants from Leicester City Council, Man Power Service Commission and Department of Environment. Natubhai convinced Jains in India to gift the temple for the Leicester Jain Centre and established the Jinalaya Trust in India. The Pratistha Mahotsav was for 16 days starting from 8th July 1988 to 23th July 1988. In addition to the UK Jains, about 2000 Jains from India and Overseas came for the ceremony. The Leicester ceremony was published in almost all news papers in India. Jain centre had visits of many dignitaries including High Commissioners of India, Chief Minister of Gujarat, Artists and Actors. The Jain Centre was honoured by British Prime Minister John Major’s visit in 1997, the request cam from 10 Downing Street. The Royal Family visited the Leicester temple in 2000.

Natubhai considers the Development of the Jain Centre Leicester as one of his greatest achievement along with the creation of a museum and the establishment of Jain Academy

Whilst he was in Leicester, he was President for its 3 formative years of the National Council of Vanik Associations, He was founder president of the Jain Social Group, London formed in 1983 and of the Midland and the North Group formed in 1988. HE was also founder president of Leicester Vegetarian society, Vice president of Mahatma Gandhi Foundation Leicester amongst his other esteemed positions.

Natubhai appeared in the Who’s Who list of Indians in Britain, Whos Who International and American biographical International book of honours amongst 5000 personalities of the world

His retirement as President of Jain Samaj Europe was published in the local newspaper Leicester Mercury. Natubhai attended the World Council of Churches meeting at San Antonio as a Jain Consultant in 1989 and Parliament of World Religions in Cape Town in 1999

Natubhai was invited for garden party, the royal wedding of Prince Williams and Kathryn Middleton and a number of xmas dinners. Natubhai was invited to Her Majesty’s Golden Wedding Luncheon at the Banqueting House and Thanksgiving Ceremony on the Centenary Celebration of the Queen mother. The Queen and Duke of Edinburgh at Buckingham Palace invited Natubhai for a private luncheon on 14th December 2000. Natubhai was also invited to the opening ceremony of the Olympics and paralympics in 2010.

Tony Blair invited Natubhai for the golden jubilee celebration of her majesty’s coronation.

Natubhai was honoured with an MBE in 2012 by HM the Queen for his services to the Jain community and Interfaith relations.

The Book


  • Volume 1 And Volume 2

Natubhai is the author of "Jainism: The World of Conquerors - 2 Volumes. Whilst working to develop the Jain Centre, Leicester, he realised there was a big gap in knowledge about Jainism in general public.

This prompted him to write the 700 page, 2 volume book. The book details all aspects of Jainism, its history, culture, values and explanation of the 4 pillars of Jainism. Dr LM Singhvi, retired high commission of India said, the 2 volumes provide a comprehensive framework of Jain beliefs, Jain social and cultural ethos and the contemporary relevance of Jainism.

Natubhai was a Honorary Professor of faculty of comparative religions, Antwerp, Belgium and thought in Antwerp for 12 years for 2 weeks/year.

Current Mission

Natubhai is presently involved in the development of much needed magnificent ​ purpose built, 30,000 sq ft Jain Centre in Colindale, London at the estimated cost of £10 million. It has blessings of prominent Jain Saints, Jain and interfaith leaders​, local and national government and civic organisations.​ Its mission is improvement of spiritual and physical quality of Jain and wider community. It will be an architectural splendour of white marble, will include a place of worship and study for all major Jain traditions, world’s first Aum, Hrim shaped 108 Parshwanath Jinalay, a multifaith centre, library and a community centre.

The fully developed Jain Centre will be a global hub to provide international welfare for all by organising activities and programmes to promote its mission, based on Indian culture – the Jain way of Life: the Art of loving, the Art of living and the Art of leaving. The centre is progressing with its construction and we need your support and donations to help complete the project.
